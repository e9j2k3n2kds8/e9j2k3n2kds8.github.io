RECOMMENDATIONS

By Joe Orange

These are not the ones everyone mentions. You won't find the same fifteen titles that appear on every "best of" list. This is a list for people who watch a lot, people who have already worked their way through the obvious and are looking for something they might have missed. Some of these are obscure. Some were buried by bad timing, a limited release, or the simple bad luck of arriving before anyone was paying attention. A few are single episodes from anthology series that most people have never heard of. The list also includes some Twilight Zone episodes, because some of them deserve to be talked about more than they are, and they fit here better than anywhere else. The only rule for making this list is that it stayed with me.

Films

Film 2018

Border

Dir. Ali Abbasi · Swedish

Weird done right. Border commits completely to its premise and never flinches, which is rarer than it sounds. The makeup is some of the best I have ever seen, not the Hollywood kind designed to be admired but the kind that makes you genuinely uneasy without knowing why. Won the Un Certain Regard award at Cannes and was nominated for an Oscar for Best Makeup. Most people still haven't seen it.

Film 2009

Gentlemen Broncos

Dir. Jared Hess

A film about a science fiction writer, made by people who clearly understand science fiction writers. A young writer attends a fantasy camp and has his story stolen by the most celebrated author in the field, played by Jemaine Clement in one of the funniest straight-faced performances of the last twenty years. From the director of Napoleon Dynamite. The film has a 6.2 on IMDb and I genuinely cannot account for that number.

TV Episodes

TV Episode 1984

Child's Play

Fox Mystery Theater (Hammer House of Mystery and Suspense)

A small number of people. One location. A problem that makes no sense and keeps getting worse. Mary Crosby, Bing Crosby's daughter, leads a cast dealing with something I won't spoil, except to say it escalates in exactly the right way. A single episode, easy to find, and one that most people have never come across.

TV Miniseries 1995

The Langoliers

Stephen King · Adapted from Four Past Midnight

Passengers on a near-empty overnight flight wake up to find the world below has gone quiet in a way that is hard to name but impossible to ignore. This is one of the earliest and best depictions of liminal dread on screen, that feeling of being somewhere that should have people in it but doesn't. Based on King's novella from Four Past Midnight. The CGI has dated badly. The atmosphere hasn't.

TV Miniseries 1999

Storm of the Century

Stephen King · Original screenplay

Unlike most King adaptations, this was written directly as a television screenplay, meaning every scene and every beat was designed for the screen from the start. A blizzard descends on a small Maine island, a stranger walks in who seems to know everything about everyone, and he wants something specific. The Pied Piper of Hamelin is an old story, and King finds the modern version of it here. Outside of serious King fans, almost no one I have spoken to has seen this.

TV Miniseries 1983

V

Dir. Kenneth Johnson · NBC

Alien visitors arrive. They look friendly. They aren't. V is a story about occupation and resistance, and the parallels to wartime Europe are not subtle and not meant to be. A ragtag group of civilians, a handful of weapons, and the conviction that it is worth fighting back. It holds up better than you might expect, and better than its reputation suggests.

TV Series 2004

Garth Marenghi's Darkplace

Channel 4 · Created by Matthew Holness & Richard Ayoade

Six episodes. That is all there is, and that is the tragedy of it. Garth Marenghi is a horror author, prolific and completely convinced of his own genius, who in the 1980s wrote, directed, and starred in his own television production that was apparently too frightening to broadcast. What you are watching is a deliberate recreation of a bad 1980s hospital horror drama, and it is absolutely committed to the joke. Most people who have seen it think about it for years afterwards.
